RECIPE FOR CHICKEN BIRYANI

Biryani is a popular and flavorful dish that originated in the Indian subcontinent.
It has gained worldwide recognition and variations. It is a one-pot rice dish made with fragrant,
long-grain Basmati rice and a combination of spices, meat (such as chicken, mutton, or goat) or vegetables,
and aromatic herbs. Biryani is known for its rich and complex flavors.

Ingredients:

For the Rice:
  1. cups Basmati rice
  2. 4 cups water
  3. 2-3 green cardamom pods
  4. 2-3 cloves
  5. 1-inch cinnamon stick
  6. 1 bay leaf
  7. Salt to taste
For the Chicken Marinade:
  1. 1.5 pounds (700g) chicken, cut into pieces
  2. 1 cup yogurt
  3. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  4. 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  5. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  6. 1 teaspoon garam masala
  7. Salt to taste
For the Biryani:
  1. 2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee
  2. 2 onions, thinly sliced
  3. 2-3 green chilies, slit
  4. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  5. 1/2 cup chopped tomatoes
  6. 1/2 cup chopped cilantro (coriander leaves)
  7. 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  8. 1/4 cup fried onions (for garnish)
  9. Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk (for color and flavor)

Instructions:

Prepare the Rice:
  • Wash the rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear.
  • In a large pot, add water, rice, cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, bay leaf,
    and salt.
  • Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 12-15 minutes
    or until the rice is 70-80% cooked.
  • It should still have a slight bite.
  • Drain the rice and set it aside.
Marinate the Chicken:
  • In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder,
    turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt.
  • Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (longer is better for more flavor).
Cook the Chicken:
  • In a large, heavy-bottomed pan, heat the vegetable oil or ghee over medium-high heat.
  • Add the sliced onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add the marinated chicken and cook until it turns white on the outside.
Layer the Biryani:
  • In the same pan, layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken.
  • Sprinkle chopped tomatoes, cilantro, and mint leaves over the rice.
  • Drizzle the saffron milk over the top.
  • Sprinkle fried onions on top for extra flavor and crunch.

Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking):

  • Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with dough to ensure no steam escapes.
  • Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes.
  • You can also place a tava (griddle) under the pan to distribute the heat evenly.

Serving:

  • Gently fluff the biryani with a fork, ensuring that the chicken and rice are evenly mixed.
  • Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) or a side salad.
  • Enjoy your homemade Chicken Biryani!
  • You can also customize it by adding vegetables, seafood,
    or even making a vegetarian version with paneer or tofu.
